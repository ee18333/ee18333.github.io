The public sector


? Positive influence ?

Not only China has involvement in the private businesses of the globe, but it also furthers its influence in the national affairs of other countries. Through the Belt and Road Initiative, China has invested vastly in Africa. There is a strong correlation between the turnover of completed projects by China in Africa and GDP per capita of Africa. The R-squared in the regression chart is 0.75. It suggests that the turnover of completed projects by China in Africa explains 75% of the variation of GDP per capita growth in Africa. But we cannot conclude that GDP growth in Africa is caused by Chinese investment in Africa because there are likely to be confounding variables.

? Negative influence ?

The Belt and Road Initiative has been criticised for dragging low-income countries into a ‘debt-trap diplomacy’. Debt of Maldives and Pakistan to China has doubled within 3 years. Africa’s debt reached 30 billion dollars in 2016 which suggests that its debt to China now after 4 years should be very high, indicated by the trend of steady increases throughout the years.
